Why KAIRO

Built for operators who treat automation as money-critical

TradingView signals become real exchange orders through one fail-closed execution path — with an AI copilot that watches the desk but never holds the keys.

⛨

Fail-closed safety gates

Every order passes a deterministic, precedence-ordered gate chain — kill switch, notional cap, trading state, idempotency. If a check fails or data is missing, no order is placed, and KAIRO records exactly which gate fired.

safety in code, never AI prose

One audited execution path

All orders flow through a single audited chokepoint. Every money-safety invariant lives in one place above the exchange adapter — there is no side door to a venue.

single chokepoint closes are never blocked

Demo-first, deliberate live arming

Every strategy starts in the exchange sandbox. Real money moves only when two independent controls agree: the strategy is set to Live and the global kill switch is explicitly released.

demo by default no implicit demo→live

Never invents success

Orders are journaled before the exchange call, and an uncertain outcome becomes UNKNOWN — never a fabricated fill or a guessed "flat." Reconciliation resolves it against the real venue state, and a restart replays the write-ahead log instead of losing alerts.

PLANNED → SUBMITTED → FILLED restart-safe

Self-hosted, fully yours

Your server, your exchange keys, your ledgers — dedicated infrastructure, not shared multi-tenant plumbing. Services bind to loopback only; the sole public surface is a Tailscale Funnel URL you control.

your keys never leave 127.0.0.1

The alert carries only the signal — buy, sell, or close. Venue, sizing, leverage, and mode all come from your strategy file, so the same alert shape works on every exchange and a webhook can never fat-finger a size.
